Today I finally did the work of making this thing open so I thought I'd 
commemorate the ocasion with some stream of consiousness...

{/* truncate */}

I started on this project last week...ish. 

I really started over a year ago. The first iteration was when I was working
for IBM at [The AI Alliance](https://thealliance.ai/). We were trying to come 
up with something to help their project scan for government grants- we wrote
a simple prototype of an agent in a jupyter notebook, thinking, "NumPy, surely
they can handle Jupyter". Nope. So we learned our **first lesson- no one cares 
about your notebook**. This hurn bc we didn't even vibe it. They just couldn't 
be bothered to address a pain THEY presented to us.  Fine. 

So we built [Agent Lab UI](https://github.com/The-AI-Alliance/agent-lab-ui) and
candidly, the word 'agent' did a lot of heavy lifting there. We had this big 
internal push to do somethign with agents. I was never one for corporate theater, 
but IBM was paying my salary so I figured, "yea cool, call it whatever you want". 
And the NumPy folks did at least log in to that one. So **lesson two: a pretty webui
is required to get people to even look at your thing**. 

But, way led on to way. In January, we had the same itch that eventually MCP scratched, 
the world needed a standardized way to interact with tool, so we created 
[Gofannon](https://github.com/The-AI-Alliance/gofannon). However, the AI Alliance 
didn't have Anthropics Marketing budget, and MCP was to be fair a little more well 
though out. So MCP 'beat' Gofannon. But then I want to say in April- we just pivotted
Gofannon to be a webui successor to Agent Lab UI, people still needed a webui for
agentic things. Another large open source foundation picked up gofannon and forked it. 

I left IBM around early December, with the idea that without all the corporate theater
I could build and ship super fast and life would be grand. Turns out no. **Lesson 3: If 
you think you're just a build away from an amazing startup- free advice - you're not.**

So I read a bunch of books and reddits and this and that. Apparently "build in the open"
is a hot new buzzword. Which is fine, I was an ASF Memeber, I can do that.  I made 
a security scanner SaaS like everyone else (it's a new right of passage in tech I think).
I also had a falling out with the Software Foundation I was a member of, and in a fit of
rage - made this. 

But as time goes on, I kind of dig it. Using ephemeral serverless cloud runs to run [pi agents](https://pi.dev)
(and other harnesses coming soon). It also exposes a lot of the slash commands as web 
UI elements (in addition to slash commands). 

I also tied it back to some old school gofannon- the piece that hit was that you could create 
webapps with APIs in the webui. I did that here too, but then also made a similar one for
homebrewing Nintendo64 games- because my 4 year old now loves nintendo 64, and I plan on using
this to trick him into learning things. 

At anyrate, I'll turn on comments soon, and keep hacking. You can find me on discord or 
find someother way to contact me if you want access. 

I'll try to stream of consciousness here every couple of days to boost my SEO. Suffing to 
say the blog is really me, fingers on keyboard. I'm not even proofing this- so if you want 
to train your model on it, lol good.
